Anticipated questions → backup slides

Q: Why MiniRocket, not the more accurate Matrix Profile? → B1
Concede the 4-pt gap (MP 79% vs 75%). Chose speed — no self-join at inference. If pushed on overfitting: agree it's a risk, point to participant-level CV + future larger dataset.
Q: Why your own classifier, not ICLabel / MARA? → B2
Lead with retrainability on the team's labels + Python-native. Concede ICLabel (~0.84) beats mine (~0.75) and DOES output probabilities — do NOT claim "no calibrated probability exists." Contribution is the workflow, not the classifier.
Q: Two experts, one session — enough? → B3
Small panels typical for design studies. Own the limits: n=2, same lab, E1 co-designed → "liking ≠ validation." Frame "combination beats sum" as a proposed guideline awaiting field deployment. Next: weeks-long deploy, independent raters, task-time vs MNE.
Q: Can you explain individual recommendations? (XAI) → B4
16 topo weights = tabular → SHAP/DiCE works. 9,996 MiniRocket feats = pooled PPV, no time axis → naïve attribution = "kernel #4471 mattered" (uninterpretable). Needs kernel→time back-projection; cite TimeSHAP, Native-Guide. Tabular side is the near-term win.
Q: Architecture / preprocessing defaults? → B5
Flask (MNE, scikit-learn, aeon, umap) + React/canvas, two Docker containers. Defaults (50/100 notch, 1–75 IIR, 250 Hz, FastICA 16 comps) tuned to the lab, all configurable. Generalisation = single paradigm so far → future work.
Q: Why red/green colours? (accessibility)
Matches European convention; concede red-green deficiency issue → configurable scheme for broader deployment (named in limitations).
Q: What would you do differently / next?
Better muscle classifier (integrate MNE find_bads_muscle), more labs/raters, quantitative task-time study vs MNE, project artefact events onto trial timeline (E1 request), undo.
